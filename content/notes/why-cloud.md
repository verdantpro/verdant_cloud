i have been avoiding learning a cloud provider for years. the heavy abstractions away from bare metal technologies always left me disgusted, but even i cannot deny the advantages of using cloud providers like google cloud and aws.

major cloud providers offer far more than a standard vps provider, and i figure it's time i actually understand what makes the cloud such a viable option. to put it simply, these companies provide managed services that replace all the bare metal software and services that i'm used to spinning up myself.

google cloud is my target; it aligns well with the technologies i enjoy working with... i prefer linux and open source software, google has excellent documentation, and the cloud native approach matches the direction i want to take my career.

to facilitate this goal, i am working through the [cloud resume challenge (google cloud)](https://cloudresumechallenge.dev/docs/the-challenge/googlecloud/).
